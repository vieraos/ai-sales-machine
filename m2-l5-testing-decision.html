Test, Kill & Scale

The 7-day rule. The kill trigger. The scale trigger. Know these three things and you'll never waste ad budget again.

Launch
ABO campaign goes live
3–4 creatives · Broad audience · Advantage+ on
↓
The only rule
Do not touch it for 7 days
No optimising, no pausing, no "just checking". Let Meta learn.
↓
Day 7 — Check your numbers
What is the ROAS on each ad?
ROAS below 1:1
Kill it
Spending more than earning. Turn it off immediately. Don't negotiate. Don't give it "one more day".
ROAS 1:1 to 2:1
Iterate
Something is working but not enough. Change ONE variable — hook only, or creative only, or copy only. Never two things at once.
ROAS above 2:1
Scale it
Winner found. Increase budget by 20% every 3 days. Create 3 variations before testing any new concepts.
⚡ The Winner Variation Rule — Do This Before Testing Anything New
Variation 1
Change the hook only
Same body copy, same CTA, same creative format. Just swap the first 3 seconds or opening line.
Variation 2
Change the creative only
Same copy. Different visual format — talking head → static, or B-roll → UGC. Same message, new fingerprint.
Variation 3
Change the copy only
Same creative. New body copy — different agitation angle, different buyer stage, slightly different CTA.
The numbers that actually matter
ROAS target
2:1+
Minimum to scale. 3:1+ = strong winner.
CPP target
≤50%
Cost per purchase ≤ 50% of front-end price
Scale increment
+20%
Budget increase every 3 days when ROAS holds
Kill threshold
$0
Spent over CPP target with zero sales → off
⚡
The mistake that bleeds budgets: Turning off ads before day 7 because "it's not working yet." Meta's learning phase needs time and data. An ad that looks dead at day 2 can turn into a winner by day 5. The 7-day rule is the single most important habit to build. Set it and leave it alone.
