The Data Stack

Four layers of data. Each one tells you something different. Together, they tell you exactly where your system is leaking.

Layer 1
📣
Traffic Data — What's bringing people in
Tells you which sources are generating volume and at what cost. Without this you can't know which ad creative or organic post to double down on.
CPL by sourceLead volume by channelAd ROASOrganic vs paid split
Layer 2
💬
Conversation Data — What's happening in the DMs
Where leads go cold. Which question kills the thread. How many conversations reach the booking link vs drop off. This is where most systems leak without realising it.
Conversation → qualified rateBooking link sent vs clickedDrop-off message
Layer 3
💰
Revenue Data — What's actually converting to cash
Connects traffic spend to actual sales. CPP, AOV, ROAS, LTV. Without this layer you can have great engagement metrics and still be running at a loss.
CPPAOVROASMonthly revenueLTV by source
Layer 4
🧠
Intelligence Layer — What patterns emerge over time
Which lead source produces the highest LTV customers? Which content angle attracts the best buyers? This is the layer that compounds — it's what Viera OS tracks and surfaces automatically.
Source → LTV correlationBest buyer profileContent → revenue attribution
What actually matters vs what distracts you
✓ Track these (revenue-linked)
Cost per purchase (CPP) — what you paid to get a buyer
ROAS — actual return on every dollar spent
Conversation → qualified rate — is the setter working?
Lead source → LTV — which source makes you the most long-term?
Booking link click → show rate — are your calls showing up?
✗ These don't pay bills (vanity)
Follower count — unless followers = buyers
Likes and comments — unless they're triggering ManyChat
Video views — unless you're tracking viewer → buyer conversion
CPM and CTR in isolation — without the sale they mean nothing
Total leads — quality over volume every time
⚡
You can't improve what you don't measure. Most business owners are drowning in vanity metrics while the real leaks go unfixed. Four numbers tell you almost everything: CPP, ROAS, conversation → qualified rate, and lead source LTV. Start there. Add complexity only when those four are locked.
