What goes in the Claude system prompt
Identity
Who Claude is playing. "You are [Name], an AI assistant representing [Business]. You speak in first person as if you are [Name]."
Voice & tone
Your actual writing style. Examples of how you talk. Direct, warm, no fluff — or however you naturally communicate. Paste 5–10 real DM examples.
Goal
What Claude is trying to achieve in the conversation. "Your goal is to qualify the lead and either book a discovery call or send the payment link for [offer] when appropriate."
Qualification criteria
Exactly what makes a hot lead. Budget threshold, business stage, problem type. Claude should ask for these naturally across 3–5 messages — not as an interrogation.
Handoff trigger
When to bring the human in. "If the lead mentions [specific situation] or expresses confusion, reply with [message] and flag for human follow-up."
What Claude handles automatically
01
Qualification
Asks 3–5 natural questions to confirm budget, business stage, problem fit. No interrogation — feels like a conversation.
02
Objection handling
"I need to think about it" / "Is this right for me?" — Claude handles first-response objections in your voice before escalating.
03
Link delivery
Sends booking link or payment link at the exact right conversational moment. Not too early. Not too late.
⚡
The system prompt is everything. A weak system prompt = generic AI responses that feel nothing like you. Spend the most time here. Paste real examples of your DM conversations. Give Claude your objection-handling language. The more context Claude has about you and your buyer, the more it sounds like you and converts like you.
